Privacy Policy

Recall: Remember Where It Is · Last updated 13 June 2026

Recall is a personal item tracker. We designed it to be private by default: the items, photos, categories, locations, reminders, and notes you create are stored on your device. This policy explains what limited data the app handles and why.

Information stored on your device

Everything you enter into Recall — item names, photos, notes, categories, location tags, reminders, and history — is stored locally on your device. We do not have access to it, and it is not uploaded to any server operated by us.

iCloud sync (optional, premium)

If you purchase Recall Premium and turn on sync, your Recall data is synced through your own private iCloud account using Apple's iCloud (CloudKit) so it stays consistent across your devices. This data resides in your personal iCloud and is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. We cannot see or access the contents of your iCloud data. Sync is off unless you enable it.

Analytics

To understand how the app is used and to improve it, Recall collects anonymous usage and diagnostic data through:

  • Google Firebase Analytics — see Firebase's privacy information.
  • TelemetryDeck — a privacy-focused analytics service; see TelemetryDeck's privacy policy.

This analytics data describes how features are used (for example: that a paywall was shown, that an item was created, or which screen was viewed) together with non-identifying properties such as your device language and region, your subscription status, a rough item-count band, and layout preferences. It does not include the contents of your items, your name, your email address, the advertising identifier (IDFA), or your location.

What we do not collect

  • We do not collect your name, email, phone number, or any account credentials.
  • We do not collect your location.
  • We do not use the advertising identifier (IDFA) and do not run ads.
  • We do not sell or share your personal data with third parties for advertising.

Purchases

Premium purchases are processed by Apple through the App Store. Payment is handled by Apple under Apple's Privacy Policy; we do not receive your payment details.

Children

Recall is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Your choices

You can stop all iCloud sync by turning it off in the app or in your device's iCloud settings. You can limit analytics at the system level using iOS Settings › Privacy & Security. Deleting the app removes all locally stored data from your device.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
